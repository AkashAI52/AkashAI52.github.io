1. Convexity of Interior and Closure

Problem. Let $ \Omega \subset \mathbb{R}^n $ be convex. Show that $ \mathrm{int}(\Omega) $ and $ \overline{\Omega} $ are convex.

Solution.

Convexity of $ \mathrm{int}(\Omega) $:

Fix $ a, b \in \mathrm{int}(\Omega) $ and $ \lambda \in (0,1) $. Since $ a \in \mathrm{int}(\Omega) $, there exists an open set $ V_a $ such that:

$$ a \in V_a \subset \Omega $$

Define:

$$ V = \{ \lambda x + (1-\lambda)b \mid x \in V_a \} $$

Then $ V $ is open, contains $ \lambda a + (1-\lambda)b $, and satisfies $ V \subset \Omega $. Hence:

$$ \lambda a + (1-\lambda)b \in \mathrm{int}(\Omega) $$

2. Convex Functions

Problem. Let $f_i : \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ be convex.

(i) Scalar multiple

$$ f_i(\lambda x + (1-\lambda)y) \le \lambda f_i(x) + (1-\lambda)f_i(y) $$

Multiply by $\alpha > 0$:

$$ \alpha f_i(\lambda x + (1-\lambda)y) \le \lambda \alpha f_i(x) + (1-\lambda)\alpha f_i(y) $$

Hence $\alpha f_i$ is convex.

(ii) Sum of convex functions

$$ f(x) = \sum_{i=1}^m f_i(x) $$ $$ \begin{aligned} f(\lambda x + (1-\lambda)y) &= \sum_{i=1}^m f_i(\lambda x + (1-\lambda)y) \\ &\le \lambda \sum f_i(x) + (1-\lambda)\sum f_i(y) \\ &= \lambda f(x) + (1-\lambda)f(y) \end{aligned} $$

(iii) Maximum of convex functions

$$ f(x) = \max_i f_i(x) $$ $$ \text{epi}(f) = \bigcap_i \text{epi}(f_i) $$

Intersection of convex sets is convex ⇒ $f$ is convex.

(b) Counterexample:

$$ f_1(x)=x, \quad f_2(x)=x^2 $$ $$ g(x)=\min(x,x^2) $$

Choose $x_1=\frac{1}{2}, x_2=2$:

$$ g\left(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}\right) > \frac{g(x_1)+g(x_2)}{2} $$

Hence not convex.

3. Partial Minimization

Problem. Let $f(x,y)$ convex and $C$ convex. Define:

$$ g(x) = \inf_{y \in C} f(x,y) $$

Solution.

Fix $x_1,x_2$ and $\lambda \in (0,1)$.

Choose $y_1,y_2$ such that:

$$ f(x_1,y_1) \le g(x_1)+\epsilon, \quad f(x_2,y_2) \le g(x_2)+\epsilon $$

Define:

$$ y_\lambda = \lambda y_1 + (1-\lambda)y_2 $$

Then:

$$ \begin{aligned} g(\lambda x_1 + (1-\lambda)x_2) &\le f(\lambda x_1 + (1-\lambda)x_2, y_\lambda) \\ &\le \lambda f(x_1,y_1) + (1-\lambda)f(x_2,y_2) \\ &\le \lambda g(x_1) + (1-\lambda)g(x_2) + \epsilon \end{aligned} $$

Let $\epsilon \to 0$:

$$ g(\lambda x_1 + (1-\lambda)x_2) \le \lambda g(x_1) + (1-\lambda)g(x_2) $$

Hence $g$ is convex.
